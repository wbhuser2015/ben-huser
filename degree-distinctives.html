Masters of Theology (ThM) - Old Testament Exposition and Archaeology

Program Description

The Master of Theology (ThM) program at Dallas Theological Seminary is designed to provide advanced training in biblical studies, theology, ministry, and Christian leadership. Through rigorous study of Scripture in its historical, literary, and theological contexts, the program seeks to equip students for faithful ministry, teaching, scholarship, and lifelong service to the church.

While my degree program is formally a ThM in General Studies, my academic interests and coursework have focused particularly on Old Testament Exposition and Biblical Archaeology. Through the study of Hebrew Scripture, ancient Near Eastern backgrounds, archaeology, and biblical theology, I have sought to deepen my understanding of God's redemptive plan as it unfolds throughout the biblical narrative.

This emphasis has strengthened both my confidence in the historical reliability of the biblical text and my desire to teach God's Word in a way that is faithful, accessible, and transformative for the church. As I continue to grow in ministry and scholarship, I hope to use these tools to help others understand the Bible more deeply and see how the story of Scripture ultimately points to Jesus Christ.
