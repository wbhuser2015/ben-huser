Genesis Study

A study of Genesis designed for understanding creation, covenant, family narratives, and the unfolding story of God’s people.

Study Overview

This study walks through Genesis with a focus on beginnings, God’s promises, faith, and the lives of Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph.

What’s included

  • Book introduction and key themes
  • Focused study sessions on major Genesis narratives
  • Reflection questions and teaching prompts
  • Guidance for family and small group discussion

Weekly Lessons

Week 1: Meet Your Maker

Genesis 1:1–25 — God creates an ordered world for His glory and confronts false objects of worship.

Week 2: Who Are You?

Genesis 1:26–2:1 — Humanity finds identity, dignity, and purpose as God's image-bearers.

Week 3: Life in God's Garden

Genesis 2 — God's design for flourishing through rest, work, worship, and relationships.

Week 4: When the Serpent Ruled

Genesis 3:1–7 — Temptation, distrust, and humanity's attempt to define good and evil apart from God.

Week 5: East of Eden

Genesis 3:8–24 — Shame, exile, and the first promise of redemption.

Week 6: Crouching Sin, Hidden Meaning

Genesis 4:1–16 — Sin spreads beyond Eden as Cain rejects both God and his brother.

Week 7: When It Rains, It Pours

Genesis 4:16–9:29 — Humanity's corruption reaches its climax, yet God preserves a remnant through judgment.

Week 8: The Towers We Build

Genesis 11:1–9 — Humanity seeks identity apart from God, but God's redemptive plan for the nations moves forward.

Use This Study

Great for Bible study classes, discipleship groups, and individuals who want a deeper foundation in the opening book of Scripture.
