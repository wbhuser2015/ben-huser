Christian Ministry

This section will document my growth in Christian ministry through internship experiences, ministry artifacts, and reflection on how God is shaping me for faithful service.

Part I: Internship Plan

Definition of the Value

DTS values ministry that equips others for effective service emphasizing humility and sacrificial service.

DTS is committed to helping those in ministry develop a biblical philosophy of servant-hearted service as defined and modeled by Jesus Christ and other godly individuals depicted in the Scriptures. Such ministry results from the gracious working of God through a person’s identity, background, experience, training, spiritual gifting, natural talents, and the enablement of God’s Spirit.

Goals

  • By the end of the internship, I will develop and refine four Bible teaching curricula—including a Genesis 1–11 Sunday School course, one additional Old Testament course, and two New Testament courses—that can be used within the local church for discipleship and biblical instruction.
  • By the conclusion of the internship, I will strengthen my ability to equip others for ministry by designing reproducible teaching materials and training resources that enable additional teachers to lead Bible studies effectively. (Sunday School leader training help/development)
  • By the end of the internship, I will gain practical experience in servant-hearted ministry by performing admin duties and helping with various ministry events like VBS, summer Olympics, fall festival, etc.

Strategies

  • Develop complete syllabi for four courses.
  • Submit curriculum drafts for mentor review.
  • Incorporate participant feedback into Sunday school reviews.
  • Observe experienced teachers.
  • Assist in planning and organization of educational ministries at KCC.

Resources

  • Mentor pastors and teachers
  • DTS coursework and lecture notes
  • Existing church curriculum materials
  • Genesis course notes

Measurements

  • Four completed course syllabi.
  • Written feedback on curriculum.
  • Participant surveys for each course conducted.
  • Admin duties performed for various ministry activities and feedback garnered from mentors.
