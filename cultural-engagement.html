Cultural Engagement

DTS values the cultivation of knowledge and skills that enable high awareness and effective navigation of cross-cultural dynamics resulting in effective contextualization and service in a variety of cultural contexts.

Part I: Internship Plan

Definition of the Value

DTS values the cultivation of knowledge and skills that enable high awareness and effective navigation of cross-cultural dynamics resulting in effective contextualization and service in a variety of cultural contexts.

Goals

  • By the end of the internship, I will grow in cultural awareness and personal ministry skill by getting lunch or coffee with non-members, newer attendees, or non-Christians at least three times to discuss ministry, church, faith, or spiritual questions.
  • By the end of my internship, I will grow in my service to the less fortunate by serving with Nashville Rescue Mission, a local Christian homeless ministry, at least once a semester during my internship.
  • By the end of my internship, I will grow in my ability to engage in cultural ministry by attending a conference or overseas mission to engage in cross-cultural ministry.

Strategies

  • Schedule at least three lunches or coffee meetings with non-members, newer attendees, or non-Christians to discuss ministry, church, faith, or spiritual questions.
  • Prepare thoughtful questions before each meeting that help me listen well, understand different perspectives, and learn how people experience the local church.
  • Serve with Nashville Rescue Mission at least once per semester during the internship to gain practical ministry experience among the homeless and less fortunate.
  • Attend a conference, ministry training event, or overseas mission opportunity focused on cross-cultural ministry.
  • Discuss insights from these experiences with a mentor pastor, especially how they should shape my teaching, discipleship, and ministry involvement at KCC.

Resources

  • Mentor pastors and ministry leaders
  • Conversations with non-members, newer attendees, and non-Christians
  • Nashville Rescue Mission staff, volunteers, and ministry opportunities
  • KCC outreach, discipleship, visitor follow-up, or missions materials (Scotland trip)
  • Conference, training, or overseas mission materials (Gospel Coalition conference)

Measurements

  • At least three lunches or coffee meetings completed with non-members, newer attendees, or non-Christians.
  • At least one service opportunity completed with Nashville Rescue Mission each semester during the internship.
  • Attendance at one conference, training event, or overseas mission opportunity related to cross-cultural ministry.
