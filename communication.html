Part I: Internship Plan

Definition of the Value

DTS values contextualized, effective communication of biblical and theological truth by a variety of means for personal and corporate transformation.

The seminary’s commitment to the Scriptures leads to a framework of doctrine in which the foundations of the Christian faith are affirmed and expounded. Every Alumni's ability to study the Scriptures and present biblical truth remains a core distinctive of our graduates. Effective leadership must include a passion for good communication.

Goals

  • By the end of the internship, I will develop eight Genesis 1–11 lessons that communicate biblical truth clearly, faithfully, and engagingly for adult Sunday School participants.
  • By the completion of the internship, I will teach or co-teach the Genesis 1–11 Sunday School course and incorporate feedback in order to grow as a biblical communicator.
  • By the end of the internship, I will contribute to the church podcast at least three times in order to grow in communicating biblical and theological truth through new media.

Strategies

  • Teach Genesis 1–11 course and garner feedback from participants.
  • Develop lesson manuscripts, outlines, handouts, or teaching notes for each Genesis 1–11 session.
  • Receive feedback from mentor pastors, teachers, and participants after selected teaching sessions.
  • Revise future lessons based on feedback.
  • Participate in planning, recording, or contributing content for the KCC podcast.

Resources

  • Mentor pastors and teachers
  • DTS coursework and lecture notes
  • Biblical commentaries on Genesis 1–11
  • KCC podcast team
  • Existing church podcast episodes

Measurements

  • Teach/co-teach all eight Genesis lessons.
  • Receive written or verbal feedback after at least three teaching sessions.
  • Complete all book reviews for assigned reading.
  • Participate in podcast discussions at least three times over the internship period.
