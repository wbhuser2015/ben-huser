Beliefs

A concise doctrinal synthesis drawn from my capstone paper, summarizing core Christian convictions about Scripture, God, humanity, salvation, the church, and the hope of new creation.

Brief Doctrinal Statements

The Bible

The Bible, the 66 books of the Protestant canon, is the inspired, authoritative, and inerrant Word of God. It is true in all it affirms, preserved throughout history, and sufficient for salvation and godly living. As God's revelation, Scripture is the final authority in all matters of faith and practice and the standard by which all teaching must be tested.

God

There is one true God, the creator of all things. He is eternal, all-powerful, all-knowing, and sovereign over all creation. He exists eternally in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Each person is fully God, equal in essence, distinct in person, and working in perfect unity to accomplish the divine will.

Angels, Humanity, and Sin

God created all things, including angels, who serve Him. Satan and his demons rebelled and now work against God's purposes. God created humanity in His image, male and female, to image Him and fill the earth. Humanity was created without sin but fell through disobedience, bringing sin and death to all.

Salvation

God, in His mercy, offers salvation and eternal life to sinful humanity. Before creation, He chose to adopt believers through Jesus Christ, who died as a substitute for sin. Salvation is received by grace through faith in Christ alone. Those who trust in His saving work are sealed with the Holy Spirit and kept for eternal life.

The Church and Sanctification

The church is the universal body of believers called to worship God, glorify Him, and make disciples. It exists universally and locally and observes baptism and the Lord's Supper. Sanctification is the lifelong work of the Holy Spirit, transforming believers into Christ's likeness as they grow in holiness and demonstrate true faith.

Last Things

Jesus Christ will return to judge the living and the dead and establish His eternal kingdom. There will be a bodily resurrection, final judgment, and a new heaven and new earth. God will dwell with His people forever, and there will be no more death, sorrow, or pain.

In Depth Doctrinal Statement

Revelation and Scripture

The primary way God has revealed Himself to his people today is through a collection of writings , known to the Church as the Scriptures or the Bible. These writings were penned by some 30 plus authors over a time span of about 1500 years. However, although human by authorship, the content of these writings are divine in origin, or inspired by God. God worked through the Holy Spirit , inspiring humans organically to create the divine works of Scripture. The Scriptures are verbal plenary inspiration, meaning every word of the entire Bible is from God , and thus all of the Bible is God’s word breathed out on paper . Thus, if every word is from God, then this affirms the doctrine of inerrancy: that the Bible is true in all that it affirms , for God is truth and everything He claims is true must be so. Inerrancy flows from inspiration. This inspiration applies both to the Old Testament and to the New Testament writings . The Bible is the primary and only infallible authority for the church today, but not the only authority as some might wrongly assert. Now, this might seem like a circular argument; “believe the Bible is true because the Bible tells us it is true.” While it is to some degree, this view can be held first by faith in God, that God is who He says He is , and that He has revealed himself to us through His word. The teleological argument also support validity of the Scriptures, as they, better than any other work in human history, accurately accord with and describe how and why the world functions as it does. This book alone provides sufficient answers to life’s biggest questions. The Bible then must be considered authoritative to the people of God. Since the Bible is the very words of God , then the authority of God applies to the Scriptures. The Scriptures are equivalent to God verbally speaking, and thus the same authority we would apply to God speaking directly to us, we should apply to the Scriptures themselves. God commands obedience to his word, but promises that life can be found in submitting to them. The Scriptures are also sufficient for the believer. However, they are sufficient in what they claim to be sufficient of, not for every single activity or decision. They are sufficient for the believer’s understanding of salvation, and how a sinner can be reconciled with God. They are also sufficient for those desiring to find truth and live a good and righteous life. The Scriptures are sufficient for our knowledge of Christ, and his teachings. While God can be seen outside of Scripture, His word is the primary way in which we come to know Him, and if we could not have anything but the scriptures to know Him, we would have enough. The Old and New Testament works have been preserved in both a literary and historical sense. The inspired works of God have been preserved in the literary binding we call the Canon. I Through the Holy Spirit , the church selected the works that God intended His people to have and need. The Scriptures has been preserved historically, in that although we do not have the original manuscripts of any of the biblical texts, we have faithful copies of the originals that can be trusted , and carry the same authority as the original manuscripts would have.

The Triune God

The God of the Bible, YHWH, is the one true and living God. He alone is God, and there are no others besides Him. He is eternal, infinite in power, knowledge, and wisdom, sovereign over all creation, and worthy of all worship. The Scriptures reveal that this one God exists eternally in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. Each person is fully God, equal in essence, yet distinct in personhood and role. This concept, known as the Trinity , is foundational to the Christian faith, for without it, the God of Christianity ceases to be the God of the Bible. They are not three gods, but one God, unified in essence, will, and action. This rules out both Tritheism, the false belief in three separate gods, and Modalism, the heresy that God merely appears in different forms. The early church articulated this doctrine in response to heresies such as Arianism, which denied the full divinity of Christ. Historical confessions like the Nicene Creed affirm that the Son is homoousios—of the same essence as the Father—rejecting the idea that He was created by God the Father. God the Father is the source of all things. He is unbegotten, eternal, and sovereign, the one from whom all creation exists and to whom all things will return. He is the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ in a unique, eternal relationship, distinct from His role as the Creator of all people. The Father sent the Son into the world for the redemption of sinners and raised Him from the dead. He is the one to whom prayers are directed and He holds His people securely in His hand. Jesus Christ, the eternal Son of God, is fully divine, co-equal and co-eternal with the Father. He is “the image of the invisible God” and “the exact imprint of His nature”. Through Him, all things were created, and in Him, all things are held together. The Son took on human flesh , being born of a virgin , fully God and fully man, in what is known as the hypostatic union—the joining of divine and human natures in one person. The Son was not created, nor did He come into existence at a point in time, as Arianism falsely teaches. Rather, He has always existed with the Father. His incarnation was for the purpose of redemption, serving as the substitutionary atonement for sin. By His death and resurrection, He secured eternal salvation for all who trust in Him. He now sits at the right hand of the Father, interceding for believers, awaiting the day when He will return in glory to judge the living and the dead. The Holy Spirit, the third person of the Trinity, is co-equal and co-eternal with the Father and the Son. He is not a force or an impersonal power, but a distinct person who teaches, convicts, and guides believers. The Spirit indwells all who belong to Christ, sealing them as a guarantee of their inheritance in Him. Through the Spirit, believers are empowered to put to death the deeds of the flesh and grow in Christlikeness. Lying to the Holy Spirit is equated with lying to God, demonstrating His full deity. His work is essential in the Christian life, bringing about regeneration, sanctification, and empowerment for ministry. Just as Christ was sent by the Father, the Spirit was sent by Christ to carry forward His mission, working in and through the Church. Rightly understanding the Trinity is essential to Christian orthodoxy. It safeguards the biblical portrait of God and the integrity of the gospel. To deny the Trinity is to embrace a different god than the one revealed in Scripture.

Creation and Fall

So where do we come in? The Triune God of the Bible created all that is in existence. The Scriptures open with the creation of the heavens and earth. Before the creation of the earth, God created spiritual beings known as Angels. One of these spiritual beings, commonly referred to as “Satan” throughout Scripture , revolted against God and was cast out of heaven , along with a group of other spiritual beings now known as fallen angels or demons. Today Satan and some of these demons roam the earth and contribute to the work of evil in the world , while some of the fallen angels are in imprisoned in chains until the time of judgement for committing immorality. Inversely, the subset of angels who were not cast out of heaven exist today to worship and glorify God and serve to minister to the saints who will inherit salvation. God then created the world in 6 days and his creation was totally good. At the culmination of his creation, God creates humanity imago dei. God created humans to rule and reign over the earth as his representatives. God creates humanity with both a body and spirit/soul. He created humanity in two genders, male and female. He also created them for community with one another. In the garden God ordained the covenant marriage, which is to be between one man and one woman. Humanity was created without a sinful nature , but given the choice to walk with God or rebel against him. They were created to walk with God, and to enjoy eternal life with him. The greatest tragedy in the Bible is the fall of humanity from this state of paradise. The first humans, Adam and Eve were given one command: to not eat of the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil. Being tempted by the serpent, Adam and Eve disobeyed the command of God and committed the first sin. As a result, Adam and Eve are exiled from the garden and the presence of God. The ramifications of this sin effect not just Adam and Eve, but all of humanity and all of creation. The consequence of death enters the world, and cascades through Adam to all humanity. The effects of sin and its pervasiveness in the human heart are passed down to all of Adams descendants through birth. This doctrine is known as original sin, or inherited sin. This doctrine rejects the heresy of Pelagianism, that Adam’s sin doesn’t not directly affect the rest of humanity. One of the primary consequences of sin is death. Sin doesn’t just result in physical death, but also spiritual death. Humans are separated from God, and spiritually dead without divine intervention. Humanity is doomed to this state of spiritual death, and is helpless to overcome it without the grace of God. This is what is known as totally depravity. Only through the work of the Holy Spirit can man be regenerated out of this death. Only through the blood of Christ can sin be absolved from the sinner. Today, the world still experiences the effects of sin in various forms: death, pain, suffering, corruption, pollution, perversion. While God is not the cause of this evil, he frequently uses it for good and for his glory. While Christians cannot completely eradicate sin from the world, we are called to fight against it, both in our own lives and in the world around us.

Salvation in Christ

The good news of the Bible is that God has not abandoned his creation, despite their sin. God seeks to restore and reconcile his lost creation to himself eternally through a process of salvation. This movement towards humanity actually begins before creation itself, when God, in love, predestines individuals to inherit salvation through the work he would eventually accomplish. This election is based on no work of the individual, rather the grace and mercy of God. This work of predestination could not be accomplished without the foreknown atoning work of God the Son, Christ Jesus. The redemption of a wayward sinner, and reconciliation to God can only truly be accomplished by God himself. Jesus, being both fully God and man, is able to claim victory over sin and death provide substitutionary atonement for the sin of all the world through his death on the cross. Christ willingly embraced death for his people, that they might find life and be reconciled to God . Because of Christ’s work on the cross, humanity is offered the forgiveness of sins , justification under God , and an eternal salvation in his presence after their death on this earth. This salvation is offered to the individual only by grace through faith , not as a result of any work of the individual. To participate in these benefits, one must simply confess faith in and believe in the work and lordship of Christ and experience conversion. This conversion process is however always preceded with God calling and acting on the heart of the individual. Once called, one can respond with faith in the gospel. Upon believing in Jesus as lord, one is instantly granted the benefits of justification and forgiveness and also experiences a union with Christ , regeneration and the indwelling and sealing of the Holy Spirit . With the indwelling Spirit, the believer is now able to walk a new life of sanctification, growing in their holiness and resemblance of Christ. While works do not contribute to our salvation, they will accompany a genuine salvation. Someone who is genuinely saved will continue in the faith until their death or until Christ’s return, and when one falls away it is evidence that they were not truly converted . It is impossible for someone who is a genuine believer in Christ to lose their eternal salvation. Upon the physical death of a believer, they are taken up to heaven to be in the presence of God until Christ’s physical return on the Last Day when believers will receive glorification into resurrected physical bodies .

The Church

God does not only give us salvation, He also gives us the holy catholic church. At his ascension, Christ commanded his disciples to multiply; to make disciples baptized in the name of the triune God and obedient to the commands of Christ. At Pentecost, the disciples of Jesus receive the promised Holy Spirit and Peter openly extends participation into the Christ-formed community. There the ‘Church’ is born. A biblical definition of the Church would be the God formed community of all true believers in Jesus throughout all time. One becomes a part of the church when saving faith is placed in Jesus and the Holy Spirit seals the individual. This community serves two primary missions; to glorify God and build up the body of Christ. The marks and practices of a local congregation should include the preaching of the Word , the administration of the ordinances, prayer, fellowship, generosity, and worship. During His earthly ministry, Christ ordained two acts to be practiced regularly by His followers: baptism and communion. Baptism is the first ordinance a believer should experience following their conversion. Baptism symbolizes a participation in Christ’s death and resurrection, impartation of the Holy Spirit and regeneration, and entrance into the covenant community of the church. Baptism should be administered after confession of faith in Jesus. Baptism does not save but baptism is a command of Christ and an act of obedience that every Christian who is able should undertake. Instituted by Christ during the Passover meal, the second ordinance, the Lord’s Supper, serves as a continual remembrance of and participation in the salvation story. Weekly administration of the Lord’s Supper aligns with the practices of the early church. Communion should only be administered to baptized believers in good standing with a bible-believing church and should not be eaten in an unworthy manner. It is clear in scripture that Christ is the ultimate authority of the universal church. However, God has also ordained human leadership to guide and govern the church until Christ’s return . Local bodies of the church are to be led and governed by elders or overseers. No one elder should have more authority than another, but one can be the presiding voice of the church. I hold that the office of Elders should be reserved for men. The second office of the church is the Deacon. Deacons serve the church by performing duties outside of pastoring. Deacons can be men or women. Throughout their lives, the individual believer experiences progressive sanctification. Despite the believer’s imparted righteousness , he retains his sin nature , which cannot be fully eradicated in this life. However, mortification of sin, obedience and holiness are commanded for the believer. Progressive sanctification then is the Christian’s movement toward perfection and holiness through the power of the Holy Spirit at work within him. With the indwelling Spirit, the believer is now able to live new life , growing in their holiness and resemblance of Christ. While the acts of sanctification do not contribute to our salvation, they will accompany a genuine salvation.

Return, Judgment, Kingdom and New Creation

Yet, the time of humanity has an expiration date. The era of this world will come to an end when Jesus Christ returns again to judge the living and the dead. During his earthly ministry, Christ proclaimed that he would come again in both power and glory. He foretold of a visible coming of the Son of Man in which He would gather His elect and execute judgement. He also urged His followers to live in readiness, emphasizing the unexpected and sudden timing of His return , and personally promised to come again to bring His people to Himself. The angels at Jesus’ ascension, along with the apostles echoed the message of Christ’s imminent return. Thus, the next thing on the biblical time table is the sudden, personal, visible, bodily return of Christ to the earth. Yet, Scripture also teaches that many signs must take place before Christ’s return. First, Christ himself taught that the gospel of the kingdom will be proclaimed through the whole world, providing testimony to the nations, before the end comes. The Scriptures, as well as the early church, also anticipated a literal figure known as the man of lawlessness to appear, marking the beginning of the end. This figure will arise as a powerful, deceptive leader who exalts himself as God, blasphemes the true God, performs false signs and wonders, persecutes the saints, and demands global worship. Empowered by Satan, he will lead a worldwide rebellion against God. Following or during the reign of this figure, Christ declared that there would be a time of great tribulation, unlike the world has seen before. After this time, the Son of Man will return. While the exact details of Christ’s return are a mystery, the Scriptures provide a few details of what to expect during this event. There will be cosmic signs , the “Sign of the Son of Man” in the sky , and then Christ will descend visibly from the sky on the clouds. Then, signaled by the sound of a great trumpet, the angels will be sent out to gather the elect. At this time, the dead in Christ will be raised in glory , and the believers who are alive will be transformed into an immortal body and caught up together to meet the Lord in the air. . Then Christ will establish His kingdom on the earth. As spoken about in the prophets, described in Revelation , and taught by the early church fathers , at the return of Christ, He will establish a literal kingdom on the earth, in which He will rule and reign with his saints for 1000 years. Satan will be bound and thrown into the Abyss and be unable to deceive the nations of the world. This millennial reign will be marked by unprecedented peace, justice, and blessing, as the world flourishes under Christ’s rule and the knowledge of the Lord fills the whole earth. After the 1000 years, Satan will be unleashed to lead one last rebellion against God and his people. However, fire will consume this rebellion and the devil will be cast into the lake of fire. Following this rebellion, a great white throne judgement will take place in which the rest of dead will be raised and all will be judged according to their deeds. Death, Hades, and those who are not found in the book of life will be cast into the lake of fire. However, those in Christ will not be condemned and instead be rewarded based on their works. The current heavens and earth will be wiped away, and God will make all things new. With death and sin being permanently destroyed , the New Jerusalem will descend from heaven, and God will freely dwell with His people. There will be no more mourning, crying, or pain anymore. In this everlasting paradise, believers can freely take of the tree of life and enjoy unbroken fellowship with God, beholding His glory and serving Him in perfect joy and peace, for all eternity.

This, in sum, is the Christian’s immeasurable hope: everlasting life in the presence of the perfect Triune God, offered only by the grace of God, and through the all-sufficient merit of our king, Christ Jesus.
